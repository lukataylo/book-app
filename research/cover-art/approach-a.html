BookApp · Generated Cover System · Approach A

Editorial Monogram

One color, one letter, one voice. The cover is a typographic object: a flat category-color field, an oversized serif monogram, and a small-caps title block ruled like a classic title page. Penguin Classics discipline, Apple restraint.

System rules

  • Grid: 240×360 canvas (2:3), 24px interior margin, hairline frame inset 14px. Fixed baselines — eyebrow 44, monogram 208, title block centered on 272, author 338. Nothing moves between titles.
  • Type scale: eyebrow 9.5px / +2.6 tracking (sans); monogram 148px serif; title 17–21px serif small caps (size set only by longest line); author 8px / +2.2 tracking. Faces: ui-serif display, -apple-system labels.
  • Deterministic recipe for a new title: hue = category; monogram = first letter of the first significant word; roman vs italic monogram = title-hash parity. That is the entire variation space — by design.
  • All marks are white at 100% or 55% opacity. Two opacity steps, no more.
