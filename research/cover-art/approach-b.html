Idea Glyphs

Every category owns one geometric mark — repeating circles for habit, concentric rings for psychology, ascending bars for business, an arch for philosophy. Warm paper field, single-color glyph, ink type. Bauhaus restraint: the glyph is the idea.

System rules

  • Grid: 240×360 canvas; glyph lives in a fixed 136×116 stage centered at (120, 150); eyebrow 44, title block centered on 270, author 331, 14px category-color foot bar. Constant paper #F7F3EB, constant ink #161514.
  • Type scale: eyebrow 9.5px / +2.6 tracking; title 19–23px serif (sentence case — ink, not color); author 8px / +2.2 tracking.
  • Deterministic recipe: glyph + hue = category (sub-glyph overrides allowed, e.g. money = stacked discs inside Business); element count and rotation are seeded by title hash within fixed bounds (e.g. 3–4 habit circles, ring count, orbital tilt, which timeline tick gets the dot). Color appears only in the glyph and the foot bar.
  • Stroke weights locked to 5–8px at canvas scale; flat caps and joins; no opacity in glyphs at all.
