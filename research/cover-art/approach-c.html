Radiant Beams

Covers that beam. A white sun over the flat category field, with a ray fan and dashed halo arcs drawn as pure strokes at stepped opacities — luminosity from geometry, not blends. Not a gradient in sight.

System rules

  • Grid: 240×360 canvas; sun fixed at (120, 124), r = 15, solid white; rays run from r 24 to r 40–72; halos at r 86 and r 101. Type grid is identical to Approach A (eyebrow 44, title centered on 276, author 338) so the imprint voice stays constant.
  • Light without gradients: exactly four opacity steps — 0.95 (long rays), 0.55 (short rays and hairlines), 0.40 (inner halo), 0.28 (outer halo). Round caps make ray ends read as glints.
  • Deterministic recipe: hue = category; ray count (24 / 28 / 32), ray-length rhythm, and halo rotation are all seeded by title hash. Two books never beam identically, but every cover shares one sun.
  • Type scale matches Approach A: 9.5px eyebrow, 17–21px serif small-caps title, 8px author.
