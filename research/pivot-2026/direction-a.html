Idea 3 of 11
Loss aversion ≈ twice the pull
We don't weigh gains and losses evenly. Research suggests the sting of losing feels about twice as strong as the joy of an equivalent gain — a quirk that quietly shapes how we price risk, hold investments, and resist change.

Once you can name it, you start to see it everywhere: the reluctance to sell a position at a loss, the over-weighting of a small downside, the fear of giving up something you only just acquired.
