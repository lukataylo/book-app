# Deep Work — Human-Written Summary Sources
**Book:** Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World  
**Author:** Cal Newport (2016)

---

## Source 1: Cal Newport Official Site — Book Overview

**URL:** https://calnewport.com/deep-work-rules-for-focused-success-in-a-distracted-world/  
**Approximate date:** January 2016 (author's announcement, maintained)

**Representative excerpt:**

> "Deep work is the act of focusing without distraction on a cognitively demanding task."

**Stylistic observations:**
- Newport leads with his own definition rather than a hook or scene — a deliberate authorial move that mirrors the book's academic-meets-practical voice; he names the concept, then defends it.
- The argument is structured as an economic thesis: deep work is simultaneously becoming rarer (due to distraction culture) and more valuable (due to knowledge economy demands) — the two-clause setup creates urgency without melodrama.
- Named endorsers (Dan Pink, Seth Godin, Adam Grant) appear as a list, clustered at the end of the page — Newport's site is more restrained about front-loading social proof than a typical publisher blurb.
- The author's own practice is cited as evidence ("Newport's personal habits"), making the overview a mild personal testimony — an academic who validates his theory with self-experimentation.

## Source 2: Publisher Description (Grand Central Publishing / Grand Central Life & Style)

**URL:** https://www.amazon.com/Deep-Work-Focused-Success-Distracted/dp/1455586692  
*(Originally from publisher; Amazon hosts the verbatim publisher description)*  
**Approximate date:** January 2016

**Representative excerpt:**

> "Deep work is the ability to focus without distraction on a cognitively demanding task. It's a skill that allows you to quickly master complicated information and produce better results in less time. Deep work will make you better at what you do and provide the sense of true fulfillment that comes from craftsmanship."

**Stylistic observations:**
- The description adds a third benefit to Newport's original thesis beyond "rare" and "valuable": fulfillment ("the sense of true fulfillment that comes from craftsmanship") — the publisher layers a happiness argument onto the productivity case, broadening appeal.
- "Distracted World" in the subtitle does rhetorical work the description leans into: the phrase positions the book as both diagnosis and cure, and the overview echoes it with repeated contrasts (focus/distraction, deep/shallow, mastery/routine).
- The two-part book structure is surfaced explicitly: Part 1 makes the case that deep work matters; Part 2 provides four rules. This structural transparency is common in how-to business books but less common in other genres.
- Short, assertive sentences dominate — no hedging, no conditionals. The description models the confident, distraction-free prose it is selling.
