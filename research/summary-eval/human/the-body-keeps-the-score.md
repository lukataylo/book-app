# The Body Keeps the Score — Human-Written Summary Sources
**Book:** The Body Keeps the Score: Brain, Mind, and Body in the Healing of Trauma  
**Author:** Bessel van der Kolk, M.D. (2014)

---

## Source 1: Penguin Random House — Publisher Description

**URL:** https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/313183/the-body-keeps-the-score-by-bessel-van-der-kolk-md/  
**Approximate date:** 2014 (publisher page, Penguin Books paperback 2015)

**Representative excerpt:**

> "Trauma is a fact of life. Veterans and their families deal with the painful aftermath of combat; one in five Americans has been molested; one in four grew up with alcoholics; one in three couples have engaged in physical violence. Dr. Bessel van der Kolk, one of the world's foremost experts on trauma, has spent over three decades working with survivors."

**Stylistic observations:**
- Opens with a stark declarative ("Trauma is a fact of life") before pivoting immediately to statistics — the effect is two-step: emotional resonance first, then empirical grounding. The statistics are chosen for scale and inclusivity, making it difficult for a reader to feel this doesn't apply to them or someone they know.
- The four-statistic litany (veterans, molestation, alcoholics, domestic violence) is structured as a social survey, casting the widest possible net across demographics — a deliberate decision to frame trauma as public health rather than niche or clinical.
- "Compromising sufferers' capacities for pleasure, engagement, self-control, and trust" — the four-item list mirrors the statutory opening, reinforcing the book's own structural logic.
- Ends on hope: "offers new hope for reclaiming lives" — after opening with the weight of prevalence and bodily reshaping, the description resolves on recovery, completing an emotional arc within ~150 words.

## Source 2: Bessel van der Kolk Official Site — Book Overview

**URL:** https://www.besselvanderkolk.com/resources/the-body-keeps-the-score  
**Approximate date:** 2014 (author's site, maintained)

**Representative excerpt:**

> "The Body Keeps the Score is the inspiring story of how a group of therapists and scientists—together with their courageous and memorable patients—has struggled to integrate recent advances in brain science, attachment research, and body awareness into treatments that can free trauma survivors from the tyranny of the past."

**Stylistic observations:**
- Frames the book as a story of a scientific community, not a lone expert — "a group of therapists and scientists" — which positions the work as a collaborative field-building narrative rather than a soloist's thesis, lending it more institutional weight.
- The word "courageous" applied to patients is notable: it locates heroism in the people studied, not the researchers, signaling the book's ethical posture — respect for the dignity of trauma survivors.
- "Tyranny of the past" is the most literary phrase across both sources — metaphor elevating trauma from clinical phenomenon to political/existential condition, reflecting the book's broader cultural ambition.
- The author's site description is longer and more narrative than the PRH blurb; it emphasizes the treatment journey and the range of therapeutic modalities (neurofeedback, theater, meditation, yoga) — a fuller map of the book's contents.
