## Issues common to all 10 packs

### 1. Meta-close formula — 10 of 10 — trivially fixable

Every summary_short ends with a sentence of the form *"The full summary [verb] [list of things it covers]."* The exact phrasing varies; the structure does not:

> *"The full summary walks through each of these ideas in depth, including the Loudcloud story…"*  
> *"The full summary unpacks each mechanism, the evidence behind it, and how the theory has weathered thirty years of argument."*  
> *"The full summary delivers the case studies behind each idea, plus Duhigg's step-by-step method…"*

In every case the final sentence directs the reader to *our product* rather than motivating them to read *the book*. This is the most consistent defect across both batches (18/18 packs now evaluated). Fix: delete the formula sentence; close on the book's sharpest implication or the open question only the book answers.

### 2. Em-dash over-density — 9 of 10 above ~3/300w threshold

Only the-innovators-dilemma (~2.6/300w) sits under the threshold. The batch mean is approximately 4.0/300w (excluding the-innovators-dilemma the mean rises to ~4.3). The worst offenders — the-hard-thing (5.3/300w) and the-psychology-of-money (6.1/300w) — are using em-dashes uniformly to attach explanatory clauses, not to mark genuine reversals. The single em-dash that deserves to stay in each pack is the one creating actual surprise or contradiction; the others are stylistic defaults. Fix: convert two-thirds to commas, colons, or new sentences; reserve one dash per 300 words for a real break.

### 3. Unconditional advocacy / no friction sentence — 8 of 10

Only the-lean-startup (4/5) and the-psychology-of-money (4/5) state a meaningful limit. The other eight advocate without qualification. The most consequential gaps: the-power-of-habit and the-one-thing both cite ego-depletion / willpower research that has partially failed replication — shipping these without a caveat is a credibility risk. the-innovators-dilemma omits all of Christensen's own later-career caveats. the-obstacle-is-the-way never mentions survivorship bias in the body text. Fix: one sentence per summary stating who the argument serves, where the evidence strains, or what is contested. The sentence does not have to be long or tentative — *"Critics have noted…"* or *"The method fits X better than Y"* is sufficient.
